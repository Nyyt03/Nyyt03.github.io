Astronomy

Astronomy is the discipline that studies celestial bodies, the structure, and development of the universe in space. The content includes the structure, properties, and operational laws of celestial bodies. Astronomy is an ancient science that has held an important position since the history of human civilization.

Mainly by observing the radiation emitted by celestial bodies to Earth, discovering and measuring their positions, exploring their motion patterns, studying their physical properties, chemical composition, internal structure, energy sources, and evolution laws.

In the long history of astronomy, with the improvement and development of research methods, astrometry, astromechanics, and astrophysics have been established successively.

Astronomical research has great practical significance for our lives and has a significant impact on human views of nature. Ancient astronomers determined time, direction, and calendar by observing the sun, moon, and other celestial bodies and phenomena. This is also the beginning of astrometry. If we start from observing celestial bodies and recording celestial phenomena, the history of astronomy has been at least five to six thousand years. Astronomy holds a very important position in the early history of human civilization. The pyramids in Egypt and Stonehenge in Europe are both famous prehistoric astronomical sites

The dream of stars inspires people's desire to explore. In the vast sky, every star is like an unknown world, waiting for us to uncover their mystery. It is this kind of unknown and exploration that drives human beings to constantly move forward to pursue higher technology, deeper cognition and broader vision.

Therefore, I praise the stars. They are not only a landscape in the sky, but also a strength and faith in our hearts.
