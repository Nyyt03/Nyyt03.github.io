Artificial satellite

Artificial satellites are unmanned spacecraft that orbit the Earth in space. Artificial satellites generally orbit the Earth according to the laws of celestial mechanics, but their actual motion is very complex due to the influence of non spherical gravitational fields, atmospheric resistance, solar gravity, lunar gravity, and light pressure on different orbits. Artificial satellites are the spacecraft with the largest number of launches, the widest range of applications, and the fastest development. The number of artificial satellite launches accounts for over 90% of the total number of spacecraft launches.

Artificial satellites can be divided into three categories: scientific satellites, technological experimental satellites, and application satellites. Science satellites are satellites used for scientific exploration and research, mainly including space physics exploration satellites and astronomical satellites, used to study the atmosphere, radiation belts, magnetosphere, cosmic rays, solar radiation, etc. of a certain planet, and can observe other celestial bodies. Most artificial satellites in the world are artificial Earth satellites, and there are also artificial Mars satellites.

Artificial satellites are divided into low orbit satellites, medium high orbit satellites, various artificial satellites, geostationary satellites, sun synchronous satellites, large elliptical orbit satellites, and polar orbit satellites according to their different operating orbits; According to their purposes, artificial satellites can be further divided into communication satellites, meteorological satellites, reconnaissance satellites, navigation satellites, geodetic satellites, interception satellites, etc. These diverse and versatile artificial satellites have made tremendous contributions to humanity.

Stars, those bright points of light twinkling in the sky, have inspired people's endless reverie and praise since ancient times. They are the poetry of the universe, the painting of the night sky, the witness of time, and the starting point of dreams.

It is this kind of unknown and exploration that drives human beings to constantly move forward to pursue higher technology, deeper cognition and broader vision.
