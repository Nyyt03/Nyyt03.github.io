Cosmic Wonder

Supernova explosion

A supernova explosion is a violent explosion experienced by certain stars towards the end of their evolution. These explosions are extremely bright, and the sudden electromagnetic radiation during the process can often illuminate the entire galaxy in which they are located, and can last for several weeks to months before gradually decaying and becoming invisible. During this period, the energy radiated by a supernova can be comparable to the total radiation energy of the Sun throughout its lifetime

black hole

Black holes are a dense celestial body predicted by general relativity and exist in space. The gravitational force of a black hole is extremely strong, causing the escape velocity within the event horizon to be greater than the speed of light. Therefore, a black hole is a celestial body with a curvature of spacetime that cannot escape light from its event horizon.

White Cave

A white cave is a strong gravitational source, and its external gravitational properties are the same as those of a black hole. A white hole can accrete surrounding matter to the boundary to form a layer of matter. The white hole theory can mainly be used to explain some high-energy celestial phenomena.
