space station

Space stations, also known as space stations or space stations. It is a manned spacecraft that operates in low Earth orbit for a long time, can be visited by multiple astronauts, and can work and live for a long time. Space stations are divided into two types: single module space stations and multi module space stations. A single module space station can be launched into orbit by a space vehicle at once, while a multi module space station is assembled in space by batches of space vehicles that send each module into orbit. All facilities necessary for human habitation are required in the space station, as it does not have the ability to return to Earth.

The characteristics of a space station are its large volume, complex structure, long orbital flight time, multiple functions, and the ability to carry out a wide range of space research projects.

One of the characteristics of a space station is its economy. For example, all space stations do not have the function of returning to the ground but instead accept astronauts for experiments in space, which can make manned spacecraft a tool for transporting only astronauts, thereby simplifying the structure of the space station, reducing its engineering design difficulty and space costs. In addition, the space station can carry or not carry passengers during operation. As long as the astronauts start and debug it, it can continue to work as usual, conduct regular inspections, and achieve results at that time. This can shorten the time astronauts spend in space, reduce a lot of expenses, and when the space station malfunctions, it can be repaired and replaced in space, extending the lifespan of the spacecraft. Increasing the usage period can also reduce space costs. Because the space station can fly for a long time (several months or years), it ensures the continuity and depth of space research work, which plays an important role in the gradual deepening of research and the improvement of research quality.
