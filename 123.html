To determine whether a substance is a celestial body, it can be summarized by "three looks": first, to see if it is a form of existence of matter in the universe. Astronomical objects

FIXED STAR

Stars are giant spheres of glowing plasma, mostly hydrogen, helium and trace amounts of heavier elements.

PLANET

A Planet is usually a body that does not emit light but orbits a star. It usually orbits in the same direction as the star it orbits.

SATELLITE

A satellite refers to a natural celestial body that orbits a planet in a closed orbit and performs periodic operations. Artificial satellites are also commonly referred to as satellites.
